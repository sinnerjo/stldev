Coach education

Practical help for St Louis coaches who want fun, engaging, long term focused environments built on clear principles.

Training session design

Principles for session planning
  • Every activity should connect to the game model or player pathway
  • Players should make decisions, not just repeat patterns
  • Most of the work should involve the ball and real game pictures
  • Challenge can rise as players understand the idea
Simple session outline
  • Arrival activity light touches and fun
  • Rondo or small sided game linked to the main theme
  • Main game bigger space or numbers with real decisions
  • Short reflection what did we notice and learn

Principles of play

With the ball
  • Play forward when it is on and supported
  • Use width and depth to create options
  • Protect the ball and keep it with purpose when space is not available
Without the ball
  • Stay compact and connected
  • Always have someone pressing the ball
  • Cover and balance behind the first defender

Coaching behaviors

How we speak and behave as coaches shapes the environment more than any formation.

On the field
  • Use clear and simple language that players understand
  • Ask questions instead of giving all the answers
  • Coach the player, not only the result
  • Stay positive and calm when mistakes happen
Around the team
  • Share clear expectations with players and families
  • Focus on effort, attitude, and growth in feedback
  • Model respect for referees, opponents, and teammates

Practice libraries

A few example themes that link directly to the philosophy and game model.

Rondos and possession games
Small numbers around a central space. These activities build first touch, decision making, and awareness of support angles.
Finishing from transitions
Games that start from recovery of the ball and move quickly to goal. Players learn to attack at speed with support and pressure from behind.
Positional games
Set ups that mirror the shapes used in matches, such as 3 v 2 or 4 v 3 in channels. Helps players link decisions to their future roles.
