Player pathway

A simple road map from the first kicks with friends to being ready for high school environments and beyond. Each stage has clear priorities that match the philosophy and game model.

Overview

Stage Age range Main focus
Discovery U5 to U7 Love the game, explore the ball, lots of play
Learning U8 to U10 Confidence on the ball, creativity, simple decisions
Growth U11 to U13 Transitional play, purposeful possession, more detail
Preparation U14 to U15 Intelligence under pressure, readiness for high school

Discovery U5 to U7

The first goal is joy. These years should feel like play, not like tryouts.

Key targets
  • Players look forward to practice and games
  • Plenty of touches and dribbling in every session
  • Simple one ball games with clear goals
  • Lots of smiles and imagination on the field
Coach priorities
  • Create a safe and fun environment
  • Celebrate effort and curiosity more than outcomes
  • Introduce very simple decision making in games that feel like play

Learning U8 to U10

Players begin to see more pictures in the game and gain confidence with the ball at their feet.

Key targets
  • Comfortable dribbling, turning, and protecting the ball
  • Basic passing and receiving in small sided games
  • Simple decisions such as when to pass, dribble, or shoot
  • Continued enjoyment and a growing love for the game
Coach priorities
  • Use games that force decisions instead of long lines
  • Keep the ball central to every activity
  • Encourage creativity and bravery in one v one situations

Growth U11 to U13

The game speeds up and team concepts start to matter more, while individual growth still leads.

Key targets
  • Understanding of basic team shape and roles
  • Use of transitional moments to attack with purpose
  • Better passing and receiving under pressure
  • Improved decision making in larger spaces
Coach priorities
  • Connect training directly to the game model
  • Continue to highlight decisions, not just outcomes
  • Balance individual work with small and large group play

Preparation U14 to U15

The focus shifts to applying intelligence, creativity, and technical quality under more pressure and stress.

Key targets
  • Players understand their role in the team and in the game model
  • They can identify strengths and areas to improve
  • They show maturity in training habits and match behavior
  • They feel ready to step into high school environments
Coach priorities
  • Prepare players for the tactical and physical demands of the next level
  • Support players in taking ownership of their development
  • Keep enjoyment and creativity alive even as expectations rise
